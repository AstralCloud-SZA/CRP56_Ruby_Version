CRP56 — Polymorphic Encryption

A ceremonial chamber for sovereign data.

CRP56 is a polymorphic encryption system that seals text, files, and entire folders behind a passphrase-derived shard cipher. Every payload is split into shards, each independently keyed, compressed with Zstd, and sealed under an HMAC integrity lock.

Encrypted files take the .crp56 extension — test1.png becomes test1.crp56 — while the original filename and extension travel inside the encrypted envelope itself, restored byte-for-byte on decryption.

The interface pairs a Ruby cryptography core with an Electron shell: the right-hand tactical rail, shard-flame progress line, and particle-backed atmosphere stay locked across every chamber.
